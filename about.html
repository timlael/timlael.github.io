Professional

 • Posted by Tim M. Lael on May 18, 2017 • 

 My professional life has always been motivated by two things - Challenge and Curiosity. When I was a child, I was constantly disassembling my toys and electronics to see what was inside and just how they functioned. That love for figuring things out, breaking them in the process and then fixing them set me off on the path that led me to where I am today.

 I began working for my grandfather on the family farm when I was only 11 or 12 years old. Besides learning to drive cars, trucks and tractors of all shapes and sizes at this young age, it fostered my curiosity in the design and function of things. Repair, rather than replacement, was a reqular part of farm equipment maintenance. Creating and fixing things became second nature. It was fun... I enojyed it... and I was good at it.

 In high school I used this passion to win the Vocational and Industrial Clubs of America (VICA) (Now Skills USA) Illinois State Championships in Diesel Equipment Technology. That win propelled me off to hone my skills at Denver Automotive and Diesel College and shortly thereafter, in 1993, landed me a job as a line technician at Saturn of Springfield Illinois. A line technician is a fancy name for a mechanic that is factory trained to repair all systems on the vehicle. This was an exciting and challenging time to be a line tech. On board diagnostics (OBD) was reaching its maturity and the second generation of OBD, OBD-II, was in its infancy. My love for fixing things and my hobby of computers had been married.
